TIDK (short for “Things I Didn’t Know”) is a series of blog posts about concepts I found difficult to understand. In each article, I take a concept and explain it in a way that allows you to reason about it intuitively. If you find it too complicated, shoot me an email specifying what part is complicated and I’ll make edits. 

I grew up thinking math was computation (starting at 5 adding shit like 1+1 =2).

Math remains pretty computational up to even college for engineers and people who just need the requirment out of the way. but math is so much more.

Math was our way of explaining the world. its the language of empire. [ancient history of math and using it for counting]

Math has grown a lot since, but here are some snippets

math studies counting (number theory)

math studies structures (like linear algebra and how it can describe nature/3d space)

math studies spaces (shapes and how they interact like dna and topology)

math studies change (calculus used in physics all the time) [tie it back to introduction].

Of course each of these can intersect (linear algebra has a geometric perspective)

Math gives us a means of rationalizing and understanding the world so that we can make our lives a little bit less hectic and understand the world we live in in a language agnostic to the world.

Don't see studying math as something you need to do. Interpret it as giving you a language and a lens that will help you gain a different insight into the problems you might solve some day.
